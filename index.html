My playbook

No one process will apply to every situation, but these are what I use as guideposts.

Understand & Strategize

Before anything can happen, we need to understand a few things first; who the users are, what the business needs are, and what direction the product should go.

Hypothesize & Research

Once we have a direction, we should fill in the gaps on what we know about the user. We should get to know their needs and pain points.

Design Plan

We should plan with stakeholders and let everyone know who is doing what and when. Part of making a plan is knowing a timeline, describing methods that will be used, any deliverables, and who needs those deliverables; what user research needs to be done, visual design, and any hand-off.

Prototype

We need prototypes to be able to show users in a usability test and have them click through. However coming up with a good flow, both user and task flow, are very important to do before we even create the first wireframe. This is a good opportunity to have both Product and developers become part of the process.

Validate

By now we should have done several usability tests because it should have happened throughout the process. We might need to just see if users "get it", or see how something makes them feel. Everything before validating is just assumptions. Validating a design gives us the data we need to know we are heading in the right direction.

Design System

Design systems — including style guides, principles, and interaction design patterns and code included along with a prototyping tool component library — are necessary. They allow designers and developers to build in a modular fashion with efficiency. They also help users to understand how to use the product by utilizing repeatable patterns.
